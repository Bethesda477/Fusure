About the Course: The Architect of the Digital World

The Bachelor of Computer Science (BCompSc) is a rigorous degree focused on the theoretical foundations of computation and the practical techniques used to design, implement, and manage software and hardware systems. Practical Details & Entry Requirements

Malaysia University Entry Requirements expand_more

Admission typically requires a strong performance in a recognised pre-university or equivalent program, with a compulsory credit in Mathematics at the SPM/O-Level level.

  • STPM: Minimum CGPA of 2.50 (or equivalent) with passes in 2 relevant subjects, including Mathematics.
  • A-Levels: Minimum 2 Principal Passes (or equivalent 240 UCAS points) with a pass in Mathematics.
  • UEC (Unified Examination Certificate): Minimum 5 subjects with B's, including Advanced Mathematics and a credit in Mathematics in the SPM/O-Level equivalent.
  • Foundation/Matriculation: Minimum CGPA of 2.50 in a relevant science or engineering stream.
  • Diploma: A relevant Diploma with a minimum CGPA of 2.50 may be eligible for credit transfer.
International/Overseas University Entry Requirements expand_more

Academic Requirements:

  • GCE A-Level / IB Diploma / Equivalent : Must meet the minimum grade requirements (equivalent to 2 Principal Passes) including High-Level Mathematics or Computer Science.
  • High School Certificate (12 Years): Overall average of 70-80% (varies by country), with strong results in **Mathematics and Science/ICT**.

English Language Requirements (Non-Native Speakers):

  • IELTS: Minimum Band 5.5 to 6.0 (with no band score below 5.5).
  • TOEFL iBT: Minimum score of 70 to 80.
  • UET (Malaysian University English Test): Minimum Band 3.5 to 4.
  • Note: Requirements are often waived if previous education was conducted entirely in English.
